System approach

How I approach AI systems

I treat AI systems as workflow products, not isolated model demos. The practical work is making the system understandable, testable, and useful when people depend on it.

  • Retrieval designDesign context pipelines that expose the right evidence and make document-grounded answers easier to inspect.
  • Schema validationUse structured contracts so generated outputs can be parsed, checked, routed, and corrected before they reach users.
  • UncertaintySurface ambiguity, confidence gaps, and failure modes instead of pretending every response has equal reliability.
  • Human reviewKeep expert judgment in the loop for high-impact decisions, escalation paths, and quality feedback.
  • EvaluationMeasure behavior with task-specific tests, adversarial examples, regression checks, and production feedback.
  • Production usefulnessConnect AI outputs to real workflows, adoption constraints, latency needs, auditability, and business value.
