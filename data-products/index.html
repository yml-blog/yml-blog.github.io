Beyond dashboards

Beyond dashboards

A data product is a repeated workflow that turns data into a decision, action, or operating habit. It needs a named user, a decision cadence, trusted definitions, and a feedback loop.

AI data products

AI data products

AI data products need traceable model outputs, visible uncertainty, review paths, and enough metadata to understand which data, prompt, schema, and reviewer decision produced an output.

What to monitor

What to monitor

Track freshness, schema changes, missing values, usage, adoption, overrides, drift, correction rate, experiment impact, and recurring interpretation questions.

Topic hub

Data product writing

Data products are repeated decision workflows, not just dashboards. They need a user, a cadence, trusted definitions, instrumentation, and a feedback loop that shows whether the product changes behavior or improves decisions.

For AI data products, the bar is higher: outputs need provenance, uncertainty, correction paths, and metrics that reveal adoption and quality. The writing here connects experimentation, causal thinking, analytics, platform work, and product framing so data systems become useful operational tools.
